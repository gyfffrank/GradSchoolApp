## Current state of quantum program

### Institute for Quantum and Information Sciences (IQIS)
- Interdisciplinary institute spanning Physics and Engineering
- Research areas stated: quantum optics/photonics, AMO physics, quantum sensing/networking, quantum field theory/gravitation, quantum algorithms, 2D materials for quantum devices (iron-based superconductors for single-photon detectors)
- **Alexander Maloney** (Walters Endowed Professor of Quantum Science, 2024) — background in quantum gravity and string theory; theoretical; not aligned with Frank's experimental optics work

### New Quantum Technology Lab (ECS, 2024)
- Newly opened in the College of Engineering and Computer Science
- Hardware-focused quantum technology research
- Specific faculty not named in available data

### Department hiring priorities
- Syracuse Physics explicitly states hiring priorities include **experimental quantum optics, photonics, AMO physics, quantum sensing, and quantum networking** — the department recognizes the gap and is actively trying to fill it
- If a hire in experimental quantum optics is made before Fall 2026, a newly joined PI would be actively recruiting first PhD students (comparable to the Rivera situation at Cornell)

## Funding details
- **Years of guaranteed funding:** 4 years confirmed (TA or RA)
- **Annual stipend:** ~$29,000–$32,000 (2023–24 base was $29,000; ~30% increase from 2022; current rate for 2025–26 unconfirmed); full tuition scholarship up to 24 credits
- **Funding source:** TA Year 1; RA from Year 2 with advisor
- **International-student eligibility:** Eligible for standard TA/RA; NSF GRFP ineligible (non-citizen)
- **COL note:** Syracuse NY is one of the most affordable mid-sized cities in the Northeast; $29–32k in Syracuse is roughly equivalent to $45k in Boston for purchasing power; housing is very inexpensive
