## Target PIs (top 3)

### Tian Zhong — Assistant Professor, Pritzker School of Molecular Engineering (PME)
- **Group:** Zhong Lab (quantum photonics, quantum networking, rare-earth quantum devices)
- **Group size:** ~4–6 students; growing group
- **Department:** PME (not Physics) — see routing note below
- **Recent achievements and papers:**
  - *Long-coherence telecom-band spin-photon interface* — Nature Communications, November 2024 — demonstrates quantum networking connections that could extend **2,000 km** using rare-earth ions at telecom wavelength; this is among the most compelling quantum networking hardware results of 2024
  - *Integrated SiC/Rare-earth photonic platform with in-situ photon-memory entanglement* — 2025 — 10 Mebits/s entanglement generation rate with >70% heralding efficiency; dramatically above prior art; directly enables quantum repeater networks
  - NSF CAREER Award (2020); **2025 Sturge Prize** (materials and optical properties of condensed matter, Condensed Matter Physics division)
- **Alumni placements:** Group growing; Zhong himself trained at MIT and Caltech; strong pedigree
- **Frank's specific hook:** Zhong's telecom-band quantum networking hardware (rare-earth ions + integrated SiC/LiNbO₃ photonics) is one of the most direct implementations of quantum internet architecture on this list. His 2024/2025 papers directly address the long-haul quantum networking problem Frank wants to solve. Frank's structured light and SLM expertise applies to the optical addressing and coherent driving of rare-earth quantum emitters. **Zhong is the most direct career-goal match at UChicago, but he is in PME — Frank must apply to PME PhD to work with him directly.**

### Jonathan Simon — Associate Professor, Physics; James Franck Institute
- **Group:** Simon Lab (photonic quantum Hall effect, Rydberg-mediated photon interactions, synthetic quantum materials)
- **Group size:** ~5–8 students; established group
- **Department:** Physics — directly accessible via Physics PhD
- **Research focus:** Engineering synthetic quantum matter using photons; photonic analogs of the fractional quantum Hall effect; strongly interacting photons via Rydberg blockade in microwave cavity QED; quantum simulation of topological phases
- **Recent papers:** Publications on photonic Laughlin states and Rydberg-mediated photon interactions; laboratory produces photonic analogs of strongly correlated condensed matter phases
- **Frank's specific hook:** Simon's photonic Laughlin state work is at the intersection of quantum optics and nonlinear dynamics — exactly where Frank's intellectual interests intersect. Building photonic analogs of quantum Hall physics resonates directly with Frank's history of building optical analogs (gravitational lensing, quantum pendulum dynamics under Galvez). Simon's microwave cavity QED platform also connects to quantum networking hardware architectures. The cross-disciplinary character of Simon's group (condensed matter physics implemented in quantum optics) mirrors Frank's history-physics double major intellectual sensibility.

### Liang Jiang — Professor, Pritzker School of Molecular Engineering (PME)
- **Group:** Jiang Lab (quantum communication, quantum error correction, quantum transduction, quantum networks)
- **Group size:** ~8–12 students; large, well-funded group
- **Department:** PME (not Physics); previously at Yale (moved to UChicago PME)
- **Recent achievements and papers:**
  - *Vacuum Beam Guide for Large Scale Quantum Networks* — Physical Review Letters, July 2024 — proposes quantum networks using vacuum-sealed tubes for long-distance qubit transport (thousands of miles); novel quantum networking architecture
  - *Passive environment-assisted quantum transduction with GKP states* — Physical Review X, April 2025 — quantum transduction between microwave and optical domains using GKP (Gottesman-Kitaev-Preskill) states; directly enables hybrid quantum networks
- **Alumni placements:** Strong — Jiang group alumni at top academic institutions and quantum companies; he is a theorist with broad collaborations
- **Frank's specific hook:** Jiang's quantum networking theory (vacuum beam guides, transduction) defines the architectural framework that Frank's experimental quantum networking career will operate within. While Jiang is primarily theoretical and Frank is an experimentalist, working with Jiang (either as primary advisor or co-advisor) would give Frank deep theoretical grounding in quantum networking architectures. **Jiang is in PME — same routing issue as Zhong; accessible via PME PhD, or potentially as a co-advisor from Physics.**

## Department routing: Physics vs. PME

**The optimal routing depends on Frank's primary PI target:**
- **Physics PhD:** Gives direct access to Jonathan Simon; Zhong and Jiang (PME) could be co-advisors but primary advisor must be Physics faculty; CQE membership enables cross-departmental collaboration
- **PME PhD:** Gives direct access to Zhong (Frank's best career-goal match) and Jiang; requires an engineering/materials framing of the SoP; different qualifying structure from Physics
- **Recommendation:** Frank should apply to **both UChicago Physics (Simon)** and **UChicago PME (Zhong)** as two separate applications. This is especially valuable given how strong Zhong's quantum networking fit is — this is one of the best quantum networking experimental PIs on the entire 28-program list. The double application adds cost but the fit justifies it.

## Program quirks
- **CQE industry pipeline is exceptional:** 30+ companies including IBM, Microsoft, Boeing, QuEra, and PsiQuantum are formally partnered with CQE — students in quantum photonics groups have direct access to internships, collaborative projects, and post-PhD placement pipelines that most academic programs cannot match
- **Argonne National Lab proximity:** Argonne (14 miles from campus) does not have NIST citizenship restrictions for Chinese nationals unlike DOE lab programs — verify specific project citizenship requirements before pursuing Argonne collaborations
- **Hyde Park culture:** UChicago's Hyde Park campus is described as intensely intellectual but somewhat isolated from broader Chicago; good for focused research, less so for urban quality of life
- **GRE requirement is a constraint:** Unlike MIT, Caltech, and Princeton (GRE optional), UChicago and Stanford both still require GRE — Frank should plan GRE prep together for both schools
- **Qualifier (Physics):** Written comprehensive exam covering standard physics PhD topics; format not confirmed for current year — verify with Physics graduate coordinator
