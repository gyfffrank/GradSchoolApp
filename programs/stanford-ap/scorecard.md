## Target PIs (top 3)

### Monika Schleier-Smith — Associate Professor of Physics; IQIM-affiliated; Stanford Applied Physics
- **Group:** Schleier-Smith Lab (quantum optics, quantum many-body physics, cavity QED)
- **Group size:** ~6–8 students; established group
- **Recent achievements and papers:**
  - *Graph states of atomic ensembles engineered by photon-mediated entanglement* — Nature Physics, 2024 — generates graph states (a quantum networking resource) in atomic ensembles via photon-mediated interactions; directly relevant to quantum networking architecture
  - *Optically accessible high-finesse millimeter-wave resonator for cavity QED with atom arrays* — Physical Review Applied, 2025 (with Safavi-Naeini, Welander, Nanni) — hybrid millimeter-wave/optical cavity QED system; opens quantum transduction angle
  - Prior foundational work: spin squeezing in BECs, quantum simulation with cavity-mediated interactions
- **Alumni placements:** Strong academic placement — Schleier-Smith lab alumni at top faculty positions; strong profile for quantum networking and quantum information academic career
- **Frank's specific hook:** Schleier-Smith's generation of photon-mediated graph states connects to Frank's career goal (quantum networking requires distributed entanglement of exactly this type). The Helmholtz–Schrödinger equivalence that Frank exploited in his quantum pendulum optical analog is intellectually adjacent to the Schrödinger-like dynamics Schleier-Smith engineers in her cavity QED systems — this connection can be drawn explicitly in the SoP. However, her experiments use cold atoms in cavities, not SLMs, so the direct technical overlap is conceptual rather than instrumental.

### Jelena Vučković — Jensen Huang Professor; Director, Q-FARM; Ginzton Laboratory
- **Group:** Nanoscale and Quantum Photonics Lab (quantum photonic devices, inverse design, color centers, integrated photonics)
- **Group size:** ~12–15 students; large, high-output group
- **Recent achievements and papers:**
  - *Titanium:sapphire-on-insulator integrated lasers and amplifiers* — Nature, 2024 — on-chip Ti:sapphire lasers enabling coherent visible-light photonic circuits; key building block for quantum optical communication at visible wavelengths
  - *Emerging Quadrature Lattices of Kerr Combs* — Nanophotonics, 2024 — frequency comb dynamics in nonlinear photonic resonators
  - *Nonlinear mid-infrared meta-membranes* — Nanophotonics, 2024
  - Prior: inverse-designed quantum photonic devices, photonic crystal nanocavities, semiconductor quantum dots coupled to photonic structures
- **Alumni placements:** Excellent — Vučković group alumni at MIT, Google, PsiQuantum, IBM, Quantum Opus, top academic postdocs; her group has produced more quantum photonics faculty than almost any other single PI
- **Frank's specific hook:** Vučković's inverse-designed quantum photonic devices involve sophisticated optical field engineering — including spatially structured mode profiles — that is intellectually continuous with Frank's SLM-based phase-profile design. Her on-chip Ti:sapphire laser work directly enables quantum communication hardware. Frank's experimental background in optical design (phase profiles, Fourier optics, structured light) is transferable to Vučković's photonic device characterization work.

### Jason Hogan — Associate Professor of Physics
- **Group:** Hogan Lab (atom interferometry, quantum sensing, gravitational wave detection)
- **Group size:** ~4–6 students
- **Research:** Large-scale atom interferometers for gravitational wave detection; quantum sensors; dark matter searches; precision measurements with laser-cooled atoms
- **Frank's specific hook:** Hogan's laser-cooled atom interferometry uses precisely structured optical pulses and spatial light control — accessible from Frank's optics background. However, Hogan's work is more quantum sensing / gravitational wave detection than quantum networking, making him a secondary option if Schleier-Smith and Vučković are full. His work does connect to Frank's SLM optical analog projects through the shared theme of using optical phase control to simulate physical systems.

## Funding details
- **Years of guaranteed funding:** Full PhD duration (all Applied Physics PhD students funded from start)
- **Annual stipend:** ~$58,500/year (VPGE rate 2026–27; $14,625/quarter); RA funded by advisor from summer of Year 1; Year 1 covered by department RA
- **Funding source:** Department RA in Year 1; advisor RA from Year 2+; Stanford Graduate Fellowships and Knight-Hennessy Scholars ($85k/year, full 3 years) available for exceptional applicants — Knight-Hennessy is worth applying for separately
- **Summer support:** Included in 12-month RA
- **International-student eligibility:** Fully eligible; NSF GRFP ineligible (non-citizen); internal Stanford fellowships open
- **COL note:** Palo Alto / Stanford area is among the most expensive in the US — $58,500 in Bay Area is equivalent to roughly $38,000 in Ithaca or $44,000 in New Haven after COL adjustment; Frank should model actual living costs carefully before ranking Stanford above programs with lower nominal but higher real stipends

## Program quirks
- **Q-FARM and CQC ecosystems:** Stanford's Quantum Computation Center (CQC) and Q-FARM bridge Physics, Applied Physics, EE, and CS; Applied Physics students have access to all quantum faculty regardless of department
- **Cohort size:** Applied Physics is smaller than Physics (~10–15 students/year); more intimate than large programs
- **Knight-Hennessy Scholars:** Up to 100 fully funded graduate fellowships at Stanford ($85k+/year, 3 years) — Frank should apply separately; highly competitive but explicitly values interdisciplinary backgrounds
- **Qualifier:** Not confirmed for current AP format; verify with Applied Physics graduate coordinator
- **Vučković group rotation:** Students typically join the group directly or after rotation; Vučković is known for high-output culture; very good for students who want to move fast toward publications
