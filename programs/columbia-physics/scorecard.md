## Target PIs (top 3)

### Ana Asenjo-Garcia — Associate Professor of Physics
- **Group:** Theoretical quantum optics and quantum networks (joined Columbia 2019)
- **Group size:** ~3–5 students; primarily theory
- **Recent papers:**
  - Universal scaling laws for correlated decay in Markovian quantum systems (2024/2025) — opens directions for fault-tolerant quantum computing and precision metrology
  - Theoretical work on Dicke superradiance in extended atomic arrays — experimentally validated by other groups
  - Research on photon-mediated atomic interactions, emergent many-body quantum optics phenomena
- **Alumni placements:** Group produces theory postdocs at top institutions; specific names not verified
- **Frank's specific hook:** Asenjo-Garcia's quantum networking theory directly maps to Frank's optical networking career goal. However, her group is theory-first — Frank would need to frame his application around a theory-experiment collaboration desire, not direct experimental work. **Best approached as a co-advisor or theory complement to an experimental collaborator.**

### Michal Lipson — Eugene Higgins Professor, Applied Physics and Applied Mathematics / EE
- **Group:** Lipson Nanophotonics Group (lipson.ee.columbia.edu)
- **Group size:** Large, ~10–15 students; highly active
- **Recent papers:**
  - *Three-way multiphoton interference and unitary transformations via four-wave mixing* (2024) — scalable quantum photonic information processing in the frequency domain; directly relevant to quantum networking
  - Over 300 publications; 45+ patents; seminal silicon photonics work
- **Alumni placements:** Very strong — alumni at Intel, IBM, startups, top academic postdocs; NAS/NAE member; Lipson's group is one of the most productive in photonics
- **Frank's specific hook:** Lipson's 2024 multiphoton interference work using nonlinear photonics for quantum information processing connects to Frank's optical networking career goal; Frank's experimental instrumentation skills and SLM/optics background are relevant. Note: Lipson is in APAM/EE, not Physics — Frank should apply to Columbia Engineering or APAM to be in her department.

### Note on department routing
- **Asenjo-Garcia** is in the Physics department — apply to Columbia Physics PhD
- **Lipson** is primarily in APAM/EE — apply to Columbia Engineering (APAM) or Columbia EE PhD
- These are **separate applications**. If both PIs are of interest, Frank may need two Columbia applications. Confirm with each PI which program they prefer before applying.
