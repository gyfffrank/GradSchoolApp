## Program quirks
- **Cohort size:** CU Boulder Physics admits ~30–40 PhD students per year total; JILA-affiliated students are ~10–15 of these
- **Admit rate:** Highly competitive; not publicly stated; JILA specifically is among the most sought-after AMO programs globally
- **Qualifier exam style:** Not confirmed for current format — CU Boulder Physics historically has a written comprehensive exam covering classical mechanics, E&M, quantum mechanics, statistical mechanics, and mathematical methods; typically taken in year 1 or early year 2; verify current policy
- **Rotations vs. direct admit:** JILA students can typically contact advisors in advance and join a group early, though formal rotation is possible; Sun explicitly listed "quantum repeaters and quantum memories" as IQT talk topic, suggesting active recruitment at public events
- **JILA vs. CU distinction:** Students are formally enrolled in CU Boulder Physics PhD; they are not NIST employees; JILA affiliation comes through advisor. The institute's NIST connection is for shared facilities and funding, not student employment status.
- **National Quantum Nanofab (NQN):** $20M facility (2024 award) will provide quantum fabrication infrastructure not available at most universities — strong draw for device-oriented quantum photonics research
