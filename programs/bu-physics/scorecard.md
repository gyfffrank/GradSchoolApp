## Target PIs (top 3)

### Alexander Sergienko — Professor of ECE and Physics; Director, Quantum Photonics Laboratory; BU Photonics Center
- **Group:** Quantum Photonics Laboratory (quantum optics, quantum information, entangled-photon experiments)
- **Group size:** ~3–6 students; established group
- **Recent achievements and papers:**
  - *Higher-dimensional HOM effect with directionally unbiased linear-optical multiports* — CLEO / Quantum 2.0 conference, mid-2024 — Hong-Ou-Mandel interference in higher-dimensional optical systems
  - *Directionally unbiased nanophotonic waveguide multiports for integrated quantum photonics applications* — JOSA B, 2024 — scalable quantum photonic circuit elements for quantum information processing
  - *Grover coin construction and characterization with tabletop optics* — 2025 — optical quantum walk implementation using a tabletop setup; higher-dimensional four-port scatterer
  - *Finite-element assembly approach of optical quantum walk networks* — JOSA B, 2024
- **Alumni placements:** Not verified specifically; Sergienko is one of BU's senior quantum optics faculty; group produces quantum photonics researchers
- **Frank's specific hook:** Sergienko's quantum walk optics work (tabletop, structured optical components) is in the same experimental space as Frank's optical analog experiments under Galvez. Both involve designing structured optical setups to implement physically meaningful quantum operations. Frank's structured light + SLM expertise is transferable to Sergienko's higher-dimensional optical multiport experiments. However, Sergienko's work is not directly quantum networking — it is quantum information processing with photons in bulk optics.

### Miloš Popović — Associate Professor of ECE
- **Group:** Silicon Photonics Group (integrated photonics, optical communications, quantum photonics)
- **Group size:** ~5–8 students; active group
- **Research focus:** Silicon photonic integrated circuits; high-speed optical interconnects; quantum photonic device integration; nonlinear silicon photonics for optical communications
- **Recent papers:** Integrated photonics for optical communications; silicon microring resonators; optical interconnect scaling
- **Frank's specific hook:** Popović's integrated silicon photonics work connects directly to Frank's quantum networking career goal — integrated photonic circuits are the hardware infrastructure of quantum communication networks. Frank's experimental optics skills (phase profile, intensity measurement) are transferable to optical characterization of integrated photonic devices. Popović is less a quantum optics PI than an integrated photonics engineer, but the field overlap with quantum networking is strong.

### Luca Dal Negro — Professor of ECE; BU Photonics Center
- **Group:** Laboratory for Nanoscale Optics and Novel Photonic Materials
- **Group size:** ~5–8 students; established group (h-index 66, 17,600+ citations, 250+ papers)
- **Recent achievements:**
  - **$450K Army Research Office grant** — quantum photonic sensing and detection improvements via nonlinear nanostructures; active funded research in quantum photonics
  - *Cavity-enhanced light-matter interaction in Vogel-spiral devices as a platform for quantum photonics* — Applied Physics Letters, 2021 — Vogel-spiral photonic structures for quantum emitter enhancement
  - Long track record in nanophotonics, photonic quasicrystals, nonlinear optical materials
- **Frank's specific hook:** Dal Negro's quantum photonic sensing via nonlinear nanostructures intersects with Frank's interest in nonlinear optics and structured photonic platforms. His Vogel-spiral work (aperiodic photonic structures with designed light-matter interaction) resonates with Frank's phase-profile-designed optical analogs. Less direct for quantum networking, but a technically deep quantum photonics group with active funding. Best approached as a secondary option if Sergienko's group is full.
