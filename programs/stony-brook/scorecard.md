## Target PIs (top 3)

### Eden Figueroa — Professor of Physics and Astronomy; C2QA Affiliate
- **Group:** Figueroa Lab (quantum networks, quantum memory, atomic ensembles)
- **Group size:** ~4–6 students; established group
- **Research focus:** Quantum memory using warm atomic vapor cells (rubidium, cesium) and electromagnetically induced transparency (EIT); quantum repeater networks; quantum communication protocols; entanglement distribution over fiber; one of the leading US quantum memory experimentalists
- **Recent papers:** Quantum repeater demonstrations using atomic ensembles; quantum networking protocols with warm vapor cells; quantum memory at room temperature and at cryogenic temperatures; publications in Physical Review Letters and Physical Review Applied
- **Why Figueroa:** Figueroa's atomic ensemble quantum memory work is a direct experimental implementation of the quantum networking hardware Frank's career goal targets — quantum memories are the core component of quantum repeaters, which are essential for long-distance quantum communication. His group is one of the few safety-tier programs with direct quantum networking relevance. Also affiliated with C2QA (Co-Design Center for Quantum Advantage) — a DOE quantum center.
- **Frank's specific hook:** Figueroa's optical addressing of atomic ensembles uses precisely structured laser beams and phase-coherent optical fields — transferable from Frank's SLM + structured light background. Frank's quantum pendulum paper (Helmholtz–Schrödinger equivalence) is adjacent to the wave-optics of atomic coherences Figueroa manipulates. The quantum networking application is a direct career-goal match.

### Thomas Weinacht — Professor of Physics and Astronomy; APS Fellow
- **Group:** Weinacht Lab (ultrafast quantum control, time-resolved spectroscopy)
- **Group size:** ~4–6 students; established group
- **Recent papers:**
  - *Long-lived electronic coherences in molecular wave packets probed with pulse-shape spectroscopy* — Physical Chemistry Chemical Physics, 2025
  - *Direct observation of entangled electronic-nuclear wave packets* — 2024
- **Frank's specific hook:** Weinacht's tailored light field control of molecular quantum states connects to Frank's structured light expertise — both involve engineering optical field shapes to control quantum dynamics. However, Weinacht's primary focus is molecular spectroscopy, not quantum networking; he is a secondary option if Figueroa's group is full.

### Dominik Schneble — Professor of Physics and Astronomy
- **Group:** Schneble Lab (ultracold quantum gases, optical lattices, atom-wave optics)
- **Group size:** ~4–6 students
- **Research:** Bose-Einstein condensates in optical lattice potentials; atom-wave scattering; quantum simulation using ultracold atoms; slow-light interactions with BECs
- **Frank's specific hook:** Schneble's optical lattice experiments use precisely structured laser standing waves to trap and control quantum gases — the optical design skills Frank has are directly applicable. His slow-light work connects to photon-matter interactions relevant to quantum memory. Accessible from Frank's experimental optics background.

## Program quirks
- **Top-25 Physics program:** Stony Brook has historically been strong in particle physics (RHIC at nearby Brookhaven National Lab) and condensed matter; AMO is a solid sub-group within a strong overall department
- **Brookhaven National Lab proximity:** BNL is 15 minutes from campus — potential for quantum sensing / AMO collaboration with national lab resources; BNL has quantum computing and sensing initiatives
- **C2QA affiliation:** Figueroa is affiliated with the Co-Design Center for Quantum Advantage (DOE funded); students in his group have access to C2QA workshops, summer schools, and collaborations
- **GRE explicitly blocked:** Unlike most programs that "don't require" GRE, Stony Brook explicitly does not accept GRE scores — don't include them in the application
