## Key facts

- **Auto-consideration:** Single PhD application can request consideration for MA as backup; indicate in personal statement; same application, no extra fee
- **Funding structure (critical distinction):**
  - PhD students: TA/RA stipend + full tuition scholarship (pay no tuition)
  - MA students: TA/RA stipend available but **no tuition scholarship** — stipend income offset by SUNY out-of-state tuition (~$12,000–$17,000/year)
  - Net position: partially funded; Frank would receive a stipend (~$20–25k) but owe tuition; may break approximately even or run a small deficit depending on TA appointment rate and tuition
- **MA options:** (a) Coursework + comprehensive exam (no thesis); (b) Thesis (advisor required; Figueroa is target); thesis preferred for PhD pathway
- **Deadline:** ~December 15 (same as PhD; single application)

## Red flags

- **Tuition is NOT waived for MA students:** Unlike the PhD, the MA does not come with a full tuition waiver; net financial position must be calculated carefully before accepting; this is a commonly misunderstood aspect of SUNY graduate funding
- Figueroa group size moderate — confirm he is recruiting MA students (not just PhD)
