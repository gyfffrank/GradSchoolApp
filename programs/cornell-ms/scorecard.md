## Key facts

- **Funding:** Cornell AEP explicitly confirms it **cannot** offer financial assistance to MS students; no TA/RA for MS students; full tuition is student responsibility
- **Cost:** Cornell Engineering tuition ~$32,000–35,000/year; 2-year thesis MS = ~$64,000–70,000 total tuition; not viable without external funding (CSC scholarship, personal savings, employer sponsorship)
- **Thesis required:** 2-year MS requires thesis demonstrating research ability and broad Applied Physics knowledge
- **Auto-consideration:** Auto-consideration from PhD application NOT confirmed; may require separate application; verify with AEP graduate admissions
- **Deadline:** ~December 15 (verify; same window as PhD)

## PhD pathway from Cornell MS

1. Thesis with Mehta → re-apply to Cornell AEP PhD (strongest path); Cornell MS students completing strong thesis with Mehta's group are well-positioned for PhD admission
2. Cornell MS → re-apply elsewhere (MIT, JILA, Maryland) with Cornell research thesis
3. This is a highly viable PhD pathway **if Frank can secure external funding** for the MS tuition

## External funding to investigate

- **China Scholarship Council (CSC):** Chinese government scholarship program that covers tuition + living expenses for Chinese citizens studying abroad at MS or PhD level; Frank should investigate CSC fellowship eligibility; a CSC award would make unfunded MS programs (Cornell, Columbia) viable
- Cornell does not award its own MS funding, but does not restrict external fellowships
