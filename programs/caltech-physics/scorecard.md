## Target PIs (top 3)

### Manuel Endres — Professor of Physics; IQIM Member
- **Group:** Endres Lab (optical tweezer quantum simulation, neutral atom quantum computing)
- **Group size:** ~6–8 students; actively publishing
- **Recent achievements and papers:**
  - *6,100-qubit optical tweezer array* — Nature (accepted/published 2024) — largest neutral-atom quantum processor demonstrated; uses precisely engineered optical trapping potentials (SLM-based) to position and control thousands of individual atoms
  - *Erasure cooling, control, and hyperentanglement of motion in optical tweezers* — 2025 — erasure-corrected quantum operations with motional hyperentanglement; foundational for fault-tolerant quantum computation
  - ARO, NSF QLCI (Quantum Leap Challenge Institute), DARPA-funded research
- **Alumni placements:** Not verified specifically; Endres joined Caltech 2017 and has multiple PhD graduates; placed at quantum computing companies and top academic postdocs
- **Frank's specific hook:** Endres's optical tweezer work relies on precisely shaped optical fields — SLMs are the central tool for creating the trapping arrays. Frank's SLM-based phase-profile design and characterization skills (Schwarzschild lensing, Fourier-plane eigenstate encoding) are directly the same toolset Endres's lab uses to generate tweezer arrays. Frank can frame his optical analog projects as proof-of-concept demonstrations of SLM-mediated field engineering at the quantum optics level. The 6,100-qubit array scale makes this the most ambitious SLM-based experiment on the planet — directly motivating Frank's application.

### Andrei Faraon — Professor of Applied Physics; Director of Kavli Nanoscience Institute (August 2025)
- **Group:** Faraon Lab (nanophotonics, quantum photonics, rare-earth ions, quantum memories)
- **Group size:** ~6–10 students; established group
- **Research focus:** Quantum memories based on rare-earth ions coupled to nanophotonic cavities; spin-photon interfaces at telecom wavelengths; quantum networks and quantum repeaters; integrated quantum photonics
- **Recent papers:** Rare-earth ion quantum memory demonstrations; nanophotonic crystal cavity coupling to Eu³⁺ and Pr³⁺ ions; quantum repeater architecture contributions
- **Alumni placements:** Strong — Faraon group alumni at top academic institutions and quantum networking companies; his work directly informs quantum repeater development
- **Frank's specific hook:** Faraon's quantum repeater work based on rare-earth ion quantum memories in nanophotonic cavities is precisely the hardware backbone of quantum networking — it solves the long-distance entanglement distribution problem that motivates Frank's networking career goal. Frank's structured optical field work (designed phase profiles, mode engineering) is applicable to the optical addressing of rare-earth ions in nanocavities. This is the most direct quantum networking PI at Caltech.

### Oskar Painter — John G. Braun Professor of Applied Physics; IQIM Member
- **Group:** Painter Lab (quantum photonics, superconducting circuits, optomechanics, quantum information)
- **Group size:** ~8–12 students; large, high-output group
- **Recent research:** Superconducting quantum circuits for quantum information; microwave-to-optical quantum transduction (like Regal at JILA — a quantum networking primitive); optomechanical quantum sensors; photonic crystal resonators
- **Frank's specific hook:** Painter's microwave-to-optical transduction work is a quantum networking primitive directly parallel to Regal's work at JILA — enabling hybrid quantum networks linking superconducting qubit nodes via optical fiber. Frank's experimental optics background applies to the optical cavity side of these transducer systems. Painter's IQIM affiliation also means Frank would operate at the center of Caltech's quantum information ecosystem.

## Funding details
- **Years of guaranteed funding:** Full PhD duration (standard for all Caltech Physics PhD students)
- **Annual stipend:** $48,244/year from September 1, 2025 ($4,020/month); increasing to $50,000/year from September 1, 2026 — confirmed; full tuition and mandatory fees covered
- **Funding source:** Fellowship, RA, TA mix by year; all Caltech physics students receive funding
- **Summer support:** Included in standard appointment
- **International-student eligibility:** Confirmed — all support independent of domestic/international status; NSF GRFP ineligible (non-citizen)
- **COL note:** Pasadena COL is moderate for California; lower than Bay Area, comparable to Cambridge; $48–50k is comfortable in Pasadena

## Application logistics
- **Deadline:** December 15 (confirmed for Caltech Physics PhD)
- **Application fee:** Not confirmed — verify on gradoffice.caltech.edu; typically $75–100
- **GRE policy:** General GRE **NOT required** (optional — scores considered if submitted but do not outweigh academic performance and research; GRE requirement was suspended 2021–22 for equity and has not been reinstated)
- **Required materials:** Transcripts, 3 LoRs, SoP, CV — standard; TOEFL/IELTS for international applicants
- **Qualifier:** Two written candidacy exams (offered October and July; up to 3 attempts; must complete by end of Year 2): Exam 1 = Classical Mechanics + E&M; Exam 2 = QM + Statistical Mechanics + Thermodynamics; then oral candidacy exam by end of Year 3

## Program quirks
- **IQIM is the heart of Caltech quantum:** All major quantum PIs (Endres, Faraon, Painter, Preskill, Kitaev theorists) are affiliated; students in any of these groups are embedded in the premier quantum information institute in physics
- **Cohort is very small:** Caltech Physics admits approximately 20–25 students/year total — intimate environment with frequent faculty contact; not a large-department experience
- **No GRE requirement is a genuine advantage for Frank:** Unlike Stanford (required) and UChicago (required), Caltech has dropped GRE entirely — reduces one application risk factor
- **DARPA-funded research culture:** Caltech quantum programs receive substantial DARPA funding — students work on ambitious, near-term-deployable quantum technology alongside fundamental research
- **Pasadena culture:** College town feel within LA metro; excellent year-round weather; proximity to JPL (quantum sensing applications) and LA tech ecosystem
