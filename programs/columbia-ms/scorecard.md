## Key facts

- **Funding reality:** APAM explicitly states doctoral candidates only receive funding; MS students pay full tuition; ~$66,000 in tuition plus NYC living costs ($25–35k/year) = ~$100k+ total cost for the MS — this is not viable without external funding
- **MS/PhD Track option:** Columbia APAM has a dedicated "MS/PhD Track Program" — Frank could apply specifically to this track, which may come with PhD-level funding; this is separate from the standard MS; verify eligibility and funding with APAM admissions
- **Research Specialization:** MS students in semesters 3–4 can take zero-tuition research courses if a faculty supervisor agrees; this partially reduces cost but not dramatically
- **Deadline:** ~December 4 (same as Columbia PhD APAM); paired application makes sense
- **Application strategy:** The Columbia APAM PhD application (already submitted) likely auto-considers for MS; verify with APAM admissions; if so, no additional application needed

## PhD pathway from Columbia MS

1. MS → re-apply to Columbia PhD (if not admitted the first time) with thesis/research experience
2. MS/PhD Track program directly (preferred over standard MS — likely funded)
3. NYC quantum industry connections during MS → PhD at neighboring institution (Yale, Princeton, Cornell) leveraging NYC network

## Red flags

- **Do not pursue unfunded Columbia MS without external funding:** At ~$100k total cost, this is not a viable backup for a student on F-1 visa without substantial personal financial resources
- **Investigate MS/PhD Track Program first:** This may be the funded path Frank is looking for; priority action is to verify this option with APAM admissions before dismissing or accepting Columbia MS
