## Key facts

- **Degree options:** QISE Track Thesis (26 units coursework + 6 units OPTI 910 thesis = 32 units) or QISE Track Report (29 units + 3 units OPTI 909 = 32 units); thesis strongly preferred for PhD pathway
- **Duration:** Typically 2 semesters for report; 3–4 semesters for thesis
- **QISE track courses:** Quantum mechanics, quantum information theory, quantum communications systems engineering, optics fundamentals, quantum computing algorithms, quantum materials — directly aligned with Frank's quantum networking career goal
- **NSF CQN connection:** Wyant College's CQN membership means Frank would have access to CQN workshops, summer schools, and collaborations with UMass, Arizona, MIT, and other CQN partners while completing the MS
- **Deadline:** ~December 10 (verify for Fall 2027)
- **Application fee:** Verify; Arizona typically ~$85

## Target PI: Cristian (Cris) Panda
- Joined Wyant College (and Arizona Physics) in 2024; building new lab
- Background: PhD Stanford; postdoc UC Berkeley — developed lattice atom interferometer with >1 minute quantum coherence
- Research: Quantum metrology and sensing with ultracold atoms/molecules; ultrafast laser control of quantum systems
- April 2025: First experimental results published (electromagnet design, first Cesium fluorescence)
- As a new PI actively building his lab, Panda is likely to be accessible for MS students willing to do research; this is the primary route to funded RA at Arizona
- Frank's hook: Panda's AMO quantum metrology work is adjacent to quantum networking (precision clocks, quantum sensors for networking timing); ultrafast laser control maps onto Frank's optical control background

## PhD pathway from Arizona Wyant MS

1. Thesis with Panda (or another Wyant faculty) → re-apply to JILA, Maryland, Rochester, MIT, or Cornell with thesis + Wyant LoR
2. Arizona Physics PhD (Wyant faculty often hold joint appointments) — internal MS-to-PhD conversion possible
3. NSF CQN contacts developed during MS → warm introductions to PhD advisors at CQN partner institutions (UMass, MIT, NIST)
4. Wyant College optics graduates are highly sought-after by top quantum photonics PhD programs — the Arizona MS carries real credential value
