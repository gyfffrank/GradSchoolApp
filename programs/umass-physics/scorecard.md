## Target PIs (top 3)

### Rajveer Nehra — Adjunct Assistant Professor, Physics; ECE (primary); joined January 2024
- **Group:** Nehra Lab (ultrafast quantum photonics, quantum information in nanophotonics)
- **Group size:** ~2–4 students (joined 2024; actively building group)
- **Background:** Postdoc at Caltech (prior to UMass); PhD from University of New Mexico; collaborations with Kippenberg (EPFL) and others
- **Recent achievements:**
  - Published in **Science** and **Nature** (confirmed; specific papers not retrieved — check Google Scholar)
  - **DARPA award: $3,438,170** (3-year) for quantum-enhanced detection systems on nanophotonic chip
  - Research focus: Scalable, ultrafast quantum information processing in nanophotonic devices at **room temperature** — circumventing the cryogenic cooling requirement that limits most quantum photonic systems; this is a significant practical advantage for quantum networking hardware deployment
- **Frank's specific hook:** Nehra's ultrafast quantum photonics at room temperature directly addresses the deployment scalability problem for quantum networking — room-temperature quantum photonic devices are necessary for practical quantum internet infrastructure. His nanophotonic platform uses structured optical modes and coherent light manipulation, directly relevant to Frank's SLM and structured light expertise. With Science/Nature publications and a $3.4M DARPA award, Nehra is punching well above the "safety program" weight — this is an exciting young group at an accessible program.

### Robert Niffenegger — Adjunct Assistant Professor, Physics; ECE (primary); NSF CAREER 2024
- **Group:** UMass+ Trapped Ion Quantum Computing and Photonics Lab
- **Group size:** ~2–4 students (recently joined from MIT Lincoln Laboratory)
- **Background:** Postdoc at MIT Lincoln Laboratory (photonic trapped-ion QPUs); demonstrated first fully photonic control of a trapped-ion qubit at MIT — key milestone for scalable ion trap quantum computing
- **Recent achievements:**
  - NSF CAREER Award (April 2024) — scalable integrated photonic systems for trapped-ion quantum processors
  - Demonstrated full photonic control of trapped-ion qubit on chip at MIT (prior to joining UMass)
  - Research: integrated photonic chips for trapped-ion QPUs; scalable qubit addressing via on-chip optics; quantum networking via photonic interconnects between ion trap modules
- **Frank's specific hook:** Niffenegger's trapped-ion + integrated photonics work connects Frank's structured light background to quantum computing hardware. The photonic interconnect work he does — linking trapped-ion modules via optical fiber using integrated photonic chips — is literally the intra-network layer of quantum networking. Frank's SLM expertise is applicable to beam-shaping and optical addressing of ion trap systems. A Niffenegger + Nehra dual-group environment would give Frank access to both room-temperature quantum photonics and trapped-ion quantum networking.

- UMass is a **core institution** in the NSF Center for Quantum Networks ($26M, 5-year renewable; led by University of Arizona)
- CQN is specifically focused on developing the quantum internet — quantum repeaters, quantum memories, quantum networking protocols
- Students at UMass in quantum networking-adjacent groups have access to CQN workshops, summer schools, collaborative experiments, and industry partnerships
- This is unusual for a safety-tier program and directly serves Frank's quantum networking career goal
