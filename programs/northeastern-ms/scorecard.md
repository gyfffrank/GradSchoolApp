## Key facts

- **Co-op:** Northeastern's co-op program is genuinely unique for a Physics MS — students can do 6-month co-op placements at quantum companies (QuEra, PsiQuantum, IBM Research, quantum startups all located in Boston/Cambridge area); co-op income is real salary, typically $25–45k/6-months in quantum tech roles; this partially offsets tuition costs
- **Duration:** ~24 months (32 semester hours); thesis track (up to 8 hours thesis) or coursework; co-op available for both tracks
- **Deadline:** **⚠️ JULY 1, 2026 for Fall 2026 entry** — this is extremely early; the Fall 2027 deadline would be approximately July 1, 2027; Frank must be aware that Northeastern's MS deadline is mid-summer, not December like most programs; start this application early
- **Target faculty:**
  - **Ilana Dimitrova** — photonic interfaces for neutral atom quantum computing and networking; experimental AMO; directly relevant to Frank's quantum networking career goal
  - **Quantum Photonics Lab** — optical frequency combs, multi-photon quantum states; relevant
  - **Liu** — ultrafast photonics, Optica/SPIE Fellow
- **Application fee:** Verify; Northeastern typically ~$100

## PhD pathway from Northeastern MS

1. Co-op at quantum company (QuEra, IBM Research) + thesis with Dimitrova → re-apply to JILA, Maryland, Cornell with strong industry + research combination
2. Boston location → attend MIT and Harvard quantum seminars; build relationships with faculty for PhD letters
3. Northeastern does not guarantee PhD conversion; external re-application is the expected path
