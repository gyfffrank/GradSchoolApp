## Program quirks
- **Cohort size:** UMD Physics admits ~30–40 PhD students per year; JQI-affiliated ~10–15 of these
- **Admit rate:** Not confirmed; highly competitive for JQI track
- **Qualifier exam style:** UMD Physics historically has a written comprehensive qualifying exam; verify current format (some programs have shifted to research-based evaluations)
- **NIST/UMD distinction:** JQI students are formally enrolled in UMD Physics or ECE; they are NOT NIST employees. NIST co-sponsorship means shared facilities and some joint advisors, but student appointments are through UMD. The key risk is only if a student's specific advisor is a NIST employee (Gorshkov) rather than a UMD faculty member with JQI affiliation (Hafezi, Waks, Schine)
- **IonQ proximity:** IonQ is headquartered in College Park — PhD students in quantum optics/photonics have unusually direct access to a leading trapped-ion quantum computing company for industry internships and post-PhD employment
- **Rotations vs. direct admit:** JQI students can contact advisors before applying; Schine in particular (new group) is likely accessible via cold email
